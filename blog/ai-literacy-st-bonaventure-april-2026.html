St. Bonaventure's AI Literacy Mandate: A Blueprint for Higher Ed

Published: April 1, 2026
By: Ben Nichols, Upstate AI
Category: Education & AI Policy

While most colleges are still writing AI policies, St. Bonaventure University just made AI literacy mandatory for every incoming freshman.

Starting Fall 2026, every first-year student will complete an AI literacy unit in their introductory seminar. Not as an elective. Not for tech majors. For everyone.

Three Things St. Bonaventure Gets Right

1. It's a Literacy Requirement, Not a Tech Requirement

They're teaching students how to think critically about AI, the same way we teach writing or math. What it is, how it works, when to trust it, when to question it.

This is the distinction that matters. They're not training coders or data scientists. They're building critical consumers of AI—people who understand what's happening under the hood, can spot when something looks wrong, and know the difference between a feature and a fundamental capability.

2. Ethics Baked In, Not Bolted On

The curriculum covers bias, privacy, authorship, and ethical decision-making. As their Dean put it:

"We want students to understand not just what AI can do, but what it should do."

That distinction—capability vs. responsibility—is what separates genuine AI literacy from "how to use ChatGPT for your homework."

3. They're Creating a Common Baseline

By 2030, every St. Bonaventure graduate will speak the same AI language. They'll ask better questions, use tools more effectively, and spot problems earlier. That's a competitive advantage for both the university and its graduates.

Why This Matters for CNY Businesses

Starting in 2030, your entry-level hires from St. Bonaventure will have baseline AI fluency.

Not "I've used ChatGPT a few times," but genuine understanding of how AI makes decisions, where bias creeps in, and how to evaluate outputs critically.

That's a game-changer for workforce development. You won't have to spend months onboarding people on AI fundamentals. They'll already know the questions to ask.

More importantly, they'll understand why it matters. They'll have thought about the ethics. They'll be skeptical in the right ways.

The Bigger Picture: Higher Education Needs This

Most universities are treating AI as something IT handles or that engineering departments study. St. Bonaventure is treating it as a core literacy—something everyone needs, like reading and math.

That's correct. AI isn't going away. It's becoming infrastructure. And infrastructure literacy matters for accountants, nurses, teachers, and managers just as much as it does for software engineers.

The question isn't whether your employees need to understand AI. The question is whether they learned it in college or whether you have to teach them from scratch.

What's Your Baseline?

If St. Bonaventure's 2030 graduates have mandatory AI literacy, what about your current workforce?

  • Do your team members understand the difference between narrow AI and general AI?
  • Can they spot bias in training data?
  • Do they know when to trust an AI output and when to verify manually?
  • Can they explain the concept of hallucination to a customer?

If the answer is "not really," you're not alone. But you're also falling behind.

Starting Your Own AI Literacy Program

You don't need to wait for your next hiring class. You can build baseline AI literacy in your current team in 4-6 weeks:

  1. Week 1-2: Foundations. How AI actually works. The math is simpler than you think.
  2. Week 3-4: Applications & Limits. What AI is good at. Where it fails. When to trust it.
  3. Week 5-6: Strategy & Ethics. How to think about AI in your industry. Risk and opportunity.

The goal: your team can have an intelligent conversation about AI, ask the right questions, and spot opportunities and risks in your business.
